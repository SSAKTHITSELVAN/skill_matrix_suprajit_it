# Understanding Key Metrics - Simple Explanation

## 1. Average Skills Per Person

### What is it?
**The average number of skills that each person in your team has.**

### Simple Explanation
Imagine you have 5 team members:
- Person A: has 8 skills
- Person B: has 6 skills  
- Person C: has 10 skills
- Person D: has 7 skills
- Person E: has 9 skills

**Total skills = 8 + 6 + 10 + 7 + 9 = 40 skills**  
**Average skills per person = 40 ÷ 5 = 8 skills per person**

### What it tells you:
- ✅ **Higher is better** - People are well-rounded
- ✅ Shows team versatility
- ✅ Helps with resource planning

### Example Interpretation:
- **Average = 5 skills:** Team is specialized (good for focused teams)
- **Average = 10+ skills:** Team is versatile (good for flexibility)
- **Average = 3 skills:** Team lacks diversity (might be a concern)

---

## 2. Average Proficiency

### What is it?
**The average skill level of a person across all their skills (on a scale of 0-100).**

### Simple Explanation
Imagine one person has these skills with scores:
- React: 85
- Node.js: 92
- Python: 70
- Docker: 60
- AWS: 78

**Total score = 85 + 92 + 70 + 60 + 78 = 385**  
**Average Proficiency = 385 ÷ 5 = 77/100**

This means: **They're pretty good overall, at an "Expert" level (76-100 scale)**

### What it tells you:
- ✅ Overall competence of the person
- ✅ How "senior" or "experienced" they are
- ✅ How much they can contribute

### Proficiency Levels (0-100 scale):
| Score | Level | What it means |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 0-25 | Beginner | Just starting to learn |
| 26-50 | Medium | Can work independently |
| 51-75 | Expert | Can teach others |
| 76-100 | Master | Complete mastery |

## How to Use These Metrics

### For Hiring Decisions:
- **Looking for specialist?** → Find person with high proficiency in 1-2 skills
- **Looking for generalist?** → Find person with high average skills count
- **Looking for leader?** → Find person with both high skills count AND high proficiency

### For Team Planning:
- **Project needs backend expert?** → Check average proficiency in backend skills
- **Need versatile team?** → Check average skills per person
- **Building new team?** → Mix of high-proficiency specialists + versatile generalists

### For Training:
- **Person has 3 skills?** → Consider breadth training
- **Person has proficiency 40?** → Consider depth training
- **Person has 8 skills at 65?** → They're ready for advanced challenges

## Formula Reference

### Average Skills Per Person
```
Average Skills = Total number of skills across all people
                 ÷ Number of people

Example: 50 skills ÷ 5 people = 10 skills per person
```

### Average Proficiency
```
Average Proficiency = Sum of all proficiency scores
                      ÷ Number of skills that person has

Example: (85 + 92 + 78 + 65) ÷ 4 = 80 (Expert level)
```

## Common Questions

### Q: What if one person has 20 skills but low proficiency?
A: They're "Jack of all trades, master of none" - good for flexibility but may struggle with complex work.

### Q: What if one person has 3 skills but high proficiency?
A: They're a specialist - great for focused projects but less flexible.

### Q: Is high average proficiency always good?
A: Not always. If everyone averages 90, you might have:
- ✅ Great technical team
- ❌ Expensive team (senior devs)
- ❌ Maybe overkill for junior projects

### Q: Should I optimize for skills count or proficiency?
A: **Both!** Ideally:
- 60-70% specialists (high proficiency, fewer skills)
- 30-40% generalists (medium proficiency, many skills)
